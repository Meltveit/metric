About Volume Units

Volume is the quantity of three-dimensional space enclosed by a closed surface. For example, the space that a substance (solid, liquid, gas, or plasma) or shape occupies or contains.

Common volume units include liters, gallons, cubic meters, and fluid ounces. Different regions often use different standard units, with the metric system (liters, milliliters) being widely used internationally, while the United States commonly uses customary units like gallons and fluid ounces.

Unit Definitions

  • Liter (L): The volume of 1 kilogram of water at 4°C. Equal to 1 cubic decimeter.
  • US Gallon (gal): Equal to 3.785411784 liters or 231 cubic inches.
  • UK Gallon (gal): Equal to 4.54609 liters, larger than the US gallon.
  • Cubic Meter (m³): The SI unit of volume, equal to 1000 liters.
  • Milliliter (mL): Equal to one-thousandth of a liter (0.001 L).
  • Fluid Ounce (fl oz): In the US system, one fluid ounce equals approximately 29.5735 milliliters.
