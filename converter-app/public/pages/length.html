About Length Units

Length is a measure of distance. In the International System of Units (SI), the basic unit of length is the meter (m).

Different length units are used in different regions of the world. The metric system (meters, kilometers) is used in most countries, while the imperial system (feet, inches, miles) is commonly used in the United States and some other countries.

Unit Definitions

  • Meter (m): The SI base unit of length, defined as the length of the path traveled by light in vacuum during a time interval of 1/299,792,458 of a second.
  • Kilometer (km): Equal to 1,000 meters.
  • Centimeter (cm): Equal to 1/100 of a meter.
  • Millimeter (mm): Equal to 1/1000 of a meter.
  • Foot (ft): Equal to 0.3048 meters.
  • Inch (in): Equal to 2.54 centimeters.
  • Mile (mi): Equal to 1,609.344 meters.
  • Nautical Mile (nmi): Equal to 1,852 meters, used in maritime and air navigation.
